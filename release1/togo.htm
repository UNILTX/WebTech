Shanghai is such a big city that you can explore different scenery in different places.

If you are visiting Shanghai for the first time, then you need to visit the Oriental Pearl Tower and Shanghai Center. The Shanghai Center is currently the tallest building in Shanghai. You can have an overview of the city from it. They are all located in Lujiazui, the CBD of Shanghai.

It's incredibly modern, fast-paced, and more of a place for people to commute to work. But it's also a must-see.

If you want to experience Shanghai from thousands years ago, although far from the city center, Zhujiajiao offers a glimpse of ancient Shanghai.



If you are curious about modern Shanghai, Shikumen architecture is a must-visit place. Most of them are in the city center, and many of them are now historic preservation buildings.

If this is not your first visit to Shanghai and you love art, I would like to introduce you to M50 Art District. Filled with art and exhibitions, it is a great place to spend time.



In a word, there are so many things to see in Shanghai, and everything is updated so fast.
But HERE I would like to introduce three places that I like and recommend: the Bund , Xintiandi and Jing 'an Temple.
