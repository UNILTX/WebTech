Shanghai has a long history and is one of the 38 historical and cultural cities in the second batch issued by The State Council in 1986. The true origin of the name "Shanghai" began in the song dynasty, at that time in Shanghai, has begun to become an emerging trading port in China, then in the Shanghai area has eighteen big pu, pu, one is called Shanghai in today to shiliupu the huangpu river, the bund at its west bank town of Shanghai, which is the origin of the name "Shanghai". In the Yuan Dynasty, The town of Shanghai had developed greatly and a city vessel was set up. Since then, Shanghai town was upgraded to Shanghai County. In Ming Dynasty, Shanghai had become the largest cotton and textile center in China and its commercial economy developed day by day. In 1685, the Qing government set up a customs office in Shanghai.
In 1843, after the Opium War, Shanghai was forced to open as a treaty port by colonialists. In 1845, The British first established a concession in Shanghai. Later, the United States established a concession in Shanghai. In 1849, France also established a concession in Shanghai. In 1863, after the Anglo-American concession was merged into the public concession, Shanghai was actually divided into three parts: Chinese Concession, public concession and French Concession. In more than 100 years after 1843, Shanghai became the main port for foreign colonialists to dump goods in China and plunder raw materials and money. Therefore, Shanghai was also known as the "paradise of adventurers". In January 1925, Fengjun entered Shanghai, and the Beijing government renamed Shanghai songshanghai. On July 7, 1927, Shanghai Special City was established. July 1, 1930 Shanghai special City renamed Shanghai. After the victory of the War of Resistance against Japanese Aggression in 1945, the concession was withdrawn. On May 27, 1949, Shanghai was liberated and became an important industrial and commercial base of new China after the long-term transformation and construction of successive Shanghai governments and people. On October 1, 1949, the People's Republic of China was founded. Shanghai is still a municipality directly under the Central Government. Shanghai is divided into 20 urban areas and 10 suburban areas.
