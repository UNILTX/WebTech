Origins of Shanghai Culture

The culture of Shanghai is called "Haipai(海派) culture". Its essence is the reference to European and American culture.
Shanghai culture is a unique cultural phenomenon of Shanghai, which is gradually formed on the basis of the traditional culture of South of the Yangtze River (Wuyue culture) and the integration of modern industrial civilization from Europe and America which has far-reaching influence on Shanghai.

Language

The local language of Shanghai is Shanghainese. When I was very young, there were many teachers in the school who taught in Shanghainese. But as Mandarin was promoted across China a decade ago, fewer and fewer Shanghai children can speak it proficiently. This is also a necessity due to the introduction of non-native populations. But I speak Shanghainese at home with my family, and I think it's also important to carry on the culture and the language.

Life Style

The pace of life in Shanghai is very fast, everyone living in Shanghai is in a hurry, rarely in Shanghai to see someone living a slow pace of life, ordinary people are working hard for life.
Although usually busy, shanghainese are extremely capable of enjoying life when they are free, even if the enjoyment is just sitting alone in a room with scented candles burning.
Besides, Shanghai is a well-behaved city, which is especially safe. Most people maintain a friendly attitude.
